# Accountability Check

You've called for an accountability check. Time to get real.

## Process

### 1. Gather Recent Data

Read:
- Last 3-7 days of journal entries from `/journal/daily/`
- Current week's habit scores from `/metrics/habits/`
- Active project files from `/projects/`
- Any recent weekly reviews

### 2. Aggressive Assessment

Analyze the data and be brutally honest:

**Commitment Tracking**
- What was committed to in morning briefs?
- What actually got done?
- What's the hit rate?

**Habit Performance**
- What's the score trending?
- Which habits keep getting missed?
- Any patterns in the misses?

**Project Progress**
- Are projects moving forward?
- What's been stalled?
- What keeps getting pushed?

**Excuse Patterns**
- What excuses have been made?
- Do they repeat?
- Are they legitimate or bullshit?

### 3. Deliver the Truth

Be direct. Use this format:

```
**Commitments Made vs. Kept**:
[Specific data]

[Which projects moved, which didn't]

[Direct statement about what's actually happening]

[Call out specific patterns, missed commitments, repeated excuses]

[Don't soften it. Be the accountability partner that's needed, not the one that's wanted.]

[One pointed question that gets to the root of what's happening]

---
```

### 4. Root Cause Dig

After delivering the assessment, push for root cause:

"That's what the data shows. Now tell me: what's really going on?"

Don't accept surface answers. Probe deeper:
- "Is it a systems problem? (Process isn't working)"
- "Is it an energy problem? (Physically/mentally depleted)"
- "Is it an avoidance problem? (Something you don't want to face)"
- "Is it a priority problem? (Actually not important)"
- "Is it a motivation problem? (Lost connection to why)"

Keep asking "why" until you get to something real.

### 5. Solution Design

Once root cause is identified:

"Okay, we've identified [root cause]. What specifically needs to change?"

Get to concrete solutions:
- What system change would help?
- What environment change would help?
- What support is needed?
- What needs to be eliminated?

### 6. Recommitment

"Based on this conversation, what's your new commitment?"

Requirements for commitment:
- Must be specific (not vague)
- Must have a timeframe
- Must address the root cause
- Must be realistic but stretching

"Say it out loud: 'I commit to [specific thing] by [specific time].'"

### 7. Document

Add to today's journal entry under "Accountability Check" section:

```markdown
## Accountability Check | [Time]

**What was called out**:
- [Issue identified]

**Root cause identified**:
[What's really going on]

**Commitment made**:
[Specific commitment with timeframe]

**Follow-up**:
[When to check on this]
```

### 8. Schedule Follow-up

"I'm going to follow up on this. When should I check in?"

Note the follow-up in the journal entry.

### 9. Close Strong

"This is exactly why you built this system. To have someone who doesn't let you off the hook. Now go execute."
